Feet, Calves & Standing

A standing practice, a foot exploration, and a way to use them.

Hey Tim, in our session on Tuesday your calves were really grumpy. I think we've talked about it before, but I know that one well. As recently as 2022 I couldn't do standing practice for more than ~3 min because of it.

What turned it around for me was learning to coordinate the intrinsic muscles of the foot — the small muscles inside the foot itself — and to differentiate them from the extrinsic muscles (the bigger ones running down from the lower leg). TLDR When the foot does its own job, the calves stop overworking to hold you up. The exploration below is in a Feldenkrais-style, and it's a big part of how I got my standing back.

How to use this

If you want to build real capacity in standing, here's what I'd suggest:

  • Set aside about 10 minutes a day for standing practice — the guided standing audio below, or self-guided. Take frequent breaks to let the activated muscles recover :)
  • Twice a week, swap the standing out for the foot exploration instead.
  • if you're feeling ambitious, Stack them: do the ~12½-minute foot exploration first, then stand afterward. Stacking them would likely make the benefit most obvious.

Then let me know how your calves feel.
